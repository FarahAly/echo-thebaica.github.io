Museum of Islamic Art

Situated in the Bab al-Khalq area of historic Cairo, the Museum of Islamic Art is universally recognized as one of the greatest and most comprehensive collections of Islamic antiquities in the world. Its galleries meticulously chronicle the evolution of Islamic art and craftsmanship from the 7th century Umayyad period all the way through to the 19th century Ottoman Empire. The museum highlights the profound mathematical precision, geometric brilliance, and intricate floral motifs that define Islamic design.The museum houses a staggering array of rare artifacts collected from across the Islamic world, spanning Egypt, North Africa, Andalusia, the Arabian Peninsula, and Iran. Visitors can view masterpieces in beautifully carved woodwork, intricately woven carpets, delicate ceramics, illuminated Quranic manuscripts, and highly detailed metalwork. Notably, the collection features rare astronomical instruments and stunning enameled glass mosque lamps from the Mamluk era.
