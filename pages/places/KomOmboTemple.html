Kom Ombo Temple

Situated directly on the banks of the Nile, the Temple of Kom Ombo is architecturally unique due to its perfectly symmetrical "double" design. Because it was dedicated to two different deities—the crocodile god Sobek and the falcon-headed god Haroeris (Horus the Elder)—the temple features twin entrances, twin hypostyle halls, twin sanctuaries, and duplicated courts. This strict symmetry ensured that neither god was favored over the other in architectural grandiosity.Beyond its unusual layout, the temple is renowned for a specific set of wall reliefs located in the rear passageway that depict an array of ancient medical and surgical instruments, including scalpels, forceps, and bone saws, highlighting the advanced medical knowledge of the era. The site also includes a nilometer used to measure river levels for taxation, and a modern Crocodile Museum nearby that houses mummified crocodiles discovered in the immediate area.
