Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)

Situated on the Giza Plateau just over a mile from the iconic pyramids, the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is the largest archaeological museum in the world dedicated to a single civilization. Designed to look like a chamfered triangle that physically points toward the pyramids, this billion-dollar, state-of-the-art facility represents a massive upgrade in how Egypt preserves and displays its ancient heritage. The sweeping entrance atrium features a colossal, 3,200-year-old statue of Ramses II that was brought here from Cairo's Ramses Square.The absolute crown jewel of the GEM's massive collection is the complete assemblage of the boy-king Tutankhamun's treasures. For the first time in history, all 5,000+ artifacts discovered by Howard Carter in 1922—ranging from his legendary gold death mask to his chariots, jewelry, and walking sticks—are displayed together in one specialized, climate-controlled exhibition space. The museum also features a grand, sloping staircase adorned with massive statues of pharaohs and gods.
