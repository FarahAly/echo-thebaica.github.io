Edfu Temple

The Temple of Edfu is widely celebrated as the most completely preserved ancient temple in all of Egypt. Constructed during the Ptolemaic Kingdom between 237 and 57 BCE, it was dedicated to the falcon god Horus. Because it was built relatively late in Egyptian history and was buried under centuries of desert sand and river silt, its roof, colossal entrance pylons, and intricate wall carvings survived millennia virtually intact. The massive entrance pylon stands an imposing 36 meters high and is flanked by majestic granite statues of the Horus falcon. Inside, the temple's walls are an invaluable source of information for archaeologists, bearing extensive inscriptions that provide crucial details on language, myth, and religion during the Greco-Roman period. The reliefs vividly depict the mythological battle between Horus and the chaotic god Set, a foundational story in ancient Egyptian theology.
