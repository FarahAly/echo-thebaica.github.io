Saqqara Step Pyramid

The Step Pyramid of Djoser, located in the vast Saqqara necropolis, is a monumental milestone in human history: it is the world's oldest large-scale stone building and the very first pyramid ever constructed. Designed by the visionary architect and polymath Imhotep during the 3rd Dynasty (around 2670 BCE), it marked a radical departure from the traditional flat-roofed mudbrick tombs known as mastabas. Imhotep essentially stacked six progressively smaller mastabas on top of one another to create the iconic stepped shape. This 60-meter-tall structure was not just a tomb but the centerpiece of a massive mortuary complex featuring courtyards, shrines, and a vast underground network of tunnels and chambers adorned with beautiful blue faience tiles. The stepped design was deeply symbolic, intended to serve as a literal stairway for the deceased pharaoh's soul to ascend to the heavens and join the sun god, Ra.
