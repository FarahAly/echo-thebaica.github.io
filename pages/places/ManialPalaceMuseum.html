Manial Palace Museum

Nestled on Rhoda Island in the Nile, the Manial Palace is an opulent, historic estate built by Prince Mohammed Ali Tewfik, the uncle of King Farouk, in the early 20th century. The palace is an architectural marvel that deliberately ignores modern Western styling, instead blending a unique, dazzling fusion of Ottoman, Moorish, Persian, and European Rococo architectural elements. The estate is surrounded by a sprawling, lush botanical garden filled with rare tropical plants collected by the prince.The palace interior is a masterclass in lavish decoration, featuring walls adorned with brilliant blue ceramic tiles, intricate carved wooden ceilings, and towering stained-glass windows. Visitors can explore the breathtaking Golden Hall, the prince's private throne room, and extensive collections of rare illuminated manuscripts, silver, and the prince's personal hunting trophies. It provides a fascinating, preserved glimpse into the extravagant lifestyle of Egypt's royal family just prior to the 1952 revolution.
