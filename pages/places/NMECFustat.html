Nat. Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC)

Located in the ancient city of Fustat (modern-day Cairo), the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization is a groundbreaking institution that differs from traditional antiquities museums. Rather than focusing solely on pharaonic times, the NMEC offers a comprehensive, chronological narrative of Egyptian history, spanning from prehistoric times through the Pharaonic, Greco-Roman, Coptic, Islamic, and modern eras. The museum's architecture is thoroughly modern, overlooking the natural Ain el-Sira lake.The museum gained massive international fame in 2021 when it became the new permanent home for 22 royal mummies—including Ramses II and Hatshepsut—who were transported there during the spectacular "Pharaohs' Golden Parade." The specialized Royal Mummies Hall is designed to evoke the solemn, subterranean atmosphere of the Valley of the Kings, utilizing dramatic, low lighting and respectful displays to house the ancient rulers.
