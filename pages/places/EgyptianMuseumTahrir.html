Egyptian Museum in Tahrir

Opened in 1902 in the heart of downtown Cairo, the Egyptian Museum in Tahrir Square is an iconic, neoclassical building that served as the world's premier repository of ancient Egyptian antiquities for over a century. Designed by French architect Marcel Dourgnon, the dusty, romantically cluttered halls of the museum evoke a deep sense of old-world archaeology. Its packed display cases and monumental statues have enchanted millions of visitors and scholars for generations.While many of its most famous artifacts—including the royal mummies and the complete Tutankhamun collection—have been strategically relocated to the newer, modernized NMEC and GEM facilities, the Tahrir museum remains an absolute treasure trove. It still houses over 120,000 profound artifacts, including the historically critical Narmer Palette, the breathtaking diorite statue of Khafre, and extensive collections of Old, Middle, and New Kingdom statuary, sarcophagi, and papyri.
