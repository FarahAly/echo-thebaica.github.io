Imhotep Museum

Positioned near the entrance to the Saqqara archaeological site, the Imhotep Museum is a modern, beautifully curated facility dedicated to honoring Imhotep, the legendary architect, physician, and vizier who designed the Step Pyramid of Djoser. Unlike the overwhelming, massive collections in Cairo, this specialized museum offers a highly focused, carefully lit display of artifacts recovered specifically from the vast Saqqara necropolis.The museum's halls feature remarkable discoveries, including the beautifully preserved wooden coffin of Imhotep himself, intricately carved Old Kingdom statues, and an array of ancient surgical and medical tools that highlight Imhotep's legacy as a pioneer of medicine. It also includes a dedicated hall honoring Jean-Philippe Lauer, the dedicated French architect and Egyptologist who spent over 70 years excavating and restoring the Saqqara complex.
