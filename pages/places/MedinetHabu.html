Medinet Habu

Located on the West Bank of Luxor, Medinet Habu is the Mortuary Temple of Ramses III and is widely considered second only to Karnak in size and architectural importance. What sets this temple apart is its heavily fortified, castle-like entrance, known as a migdol, which reflects the martial atmosphere of Ramses III's reign. The temple's massive outer walls are remarkably intact and offer a striking silhouette against the Theban mountains.The interior of Medinet Habu is celebrated for having some of the deepest, most sharply incised reliefs in Egypt, a deliberate architectural choice made by Ramses III to prevent future pharaohs from erasing his name. These walls are an invaluable historical record, most notably featuring chaotic and highly detailed scenes depicting the pharaoh's monumental naval and land victories over the mysterious "Sea Peoples." Additionally, many of the ceilings and high columns still vividly display their original, brilliant paint colors.
