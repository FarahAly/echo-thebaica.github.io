Coptic Museum

Located deep within the ruins of the Roman-era Babylon Fortress in Old Cairo (Masr el-Qadima), the Coptic Museum houses the world's most extensive and significant collection of Egyptian Christian artwork and artifacts. The museum itself is a beautiful architectural blend of Pharaonic, Greco-Roman, and Islamic influences, featuring intricately carved wooden ceilings and mashrabiya windows. It effectively bridges the historical gap between ancient Egyptian paganism and the later Islamic period.The artifacts inside trace the development of Christianity in Egypt, highlighting a unique artistic style that seamlessly blended ancient Egyptian ankhs and motifs with emerging Christian theology. Among its most priceless treasures are ancient textiles, exquisite stone carvings, and vibrant frescoes rescued from ancient monasteries. Historically, the museum is also famous for housing the Nag Hammadi library, a collection of early Christian Gnostic texts discovered in 1945 that revolutionized the understanding of early Christian history.
